Transforming University Digital Experience

Designing clarity, accessibility, and belonging across a complex academic ecosystem

UX Design Education Accessibility Drupal

Role

Product / UX Designer
UX Strategy · Information Architecture · Design Systems

Scope

University website
Multi-stakeholder platform

Overview

The Blue School redesigned its website to better serve a diverse community including prospective students, faculty, alumni, press, and industry partners.

While the school had a strong conceptual and artistic identity, its digital experience had become difficult to navigate—especially for users outside the internal community. The challenge was to translate that identity into a clear, inclusive, and scalable digital experience without losing its essence.

I led the UX work to expand an initial homepage concept into a cohesive, accessible experience across the entire site.

The Challenge

The existing website presented several usability issues:

  • Fragmented and overwhelming navigation
  • Lack of clear entry points for different user groups
  • Difficulty recognizing relevant content
  • Key information buried or hard to find

As a result, users often felt lost, unsure where they belonged, or how to complete even basic tasks.

My Role

  • UX strategy
  • Information architecture
  • User flows and wireframes
  • Design system definition
  • UI design (per sprint)
  • Accessibility considerations

I worked closely with creative, development, and product stakeholders throughout the process.

Users & Needs

Rather than designing a single generic experience, I focused on clearly defining and supporting distinct user groups:

  • Prospective students – Inspiring, accessible program information
  • Faculty & staff – Fast access to internal resources
  • Press & media – News, events, official information
  • Industry partners – Collaboration and research opportunities
  • Alumni – Staying connected and informed

These needs directly informed the site structure and navigation model.

UX Approach

Audit & Research

I audited the existing site to evaluate content structure, navigation patterns, and accessibility gaps. I also gathered user feedback to identify friction points and unmet expectations.

This phase clarified what content existed, who it was for, and why it wasn’t working.

Information Architecture

Based on research insights, I restructured the site architecture to:

  • Create clear entry points by user type
  • Reduce cognitive load
  • Improve content discoverability
  • Establish logical relationships between sections

The goal was to help users quickly understand where they belong and how to move forward.

Wireframes & User Flows

I translated the new structure into wireframes and user flows, validating navigation paths and hierarchy early in the process.

This allowed us to identify friction points before UI design and ensure consistency across different user journeys.

Design System

Instead of a static UI kit, I created a lightweight, scalable design system defining:

  • Core components (buttons, cards, headers, forms)
  • Typography scales and spacing rules
  • Color usage and interaction states
  • Accessibility standards

The system was designed to be Drupal-friendly, enabling smoother implementation and long-term scalability.

UI Design

Using the design system as a foundation, I designed UI templates incrementally per sprint.

  • Clear hierarchy
  • Consistent patterns
  • Accessibility
  • Brand alignment

The visual solution preserved the school’s minimal aesthetic while significantly improving clarity and usability.

This project reinforced the importance of early and continuous cross-team collaboration.

While the outcome was successful, closer alignment between creative, UX, and development teams earlier in the process could have strengthened decision-making and execution. Even so, every design decision remained focused on usability, accessibility, and long-term maintainability.
